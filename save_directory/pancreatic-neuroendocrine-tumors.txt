Pancreatic neuroendocrine tumors sometimes don't cause symptoms. When they do, symptoms can include:

Heartburn.
Weakness.
Fatigue.
Muscle cramps.
Indigestion.
Diarrhea.
Weight loss.
Skin rash.
Constipation.
Pain in the abdomen or back.
Yellowing of the skin and the whites of the eyes.
Dizziness.
Blurred vision.
Headaches.
Increased thirst and hunger.
